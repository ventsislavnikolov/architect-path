# Mission

Become a **Software Architect / AI Software Architect** via **internal promotion** from
Principal Software Developer.

## Why (drivers, in the learner's words)
- **Broader influence** — shape system-wide technical direction, not features on one team.
- **Title & comp** — earn the Architect level and the compensation with it.
- **Ride the AI shift** — be positioned where AI is reshaping how systems are designed/built.
- **Less coding, more design** — spend more time on trade-offs, strategy, design.

## Context
- **Path:** internal promotion (months horizon, not a job hunt).
- **Starting point:** *strong* architecture practice already — regularly designs systems,
  leads design reviews, owns architecture decisions.
- **AI interpretation:** wants **both** — (a) architecting AI systems (RAG, agents,
  inference, ML pipelines) AND (b) AI-augmented architecting (using AI to do the job).

## What "success" looks like
Promotion case is undeniable: visible org-wide influence + a portfolio of well-articulated,
defensible architecture decisions (incl. AI systems), spoken in business terms to leadership.

## Implications for teaching (Zone of Proximal Development)
The gap is **not** more architecture knowledge — it's the *operating model* of an architect:
1. Articulating decisions as explicit trade-offs in **business** language.
2. Influence without authority; the "architect elevator" (engine room ↔ board room).
3. AI-systems architecture as a set of *architectural concerns* (eval, cost, latency, guardrails).
4. AI-augmented architecting workflow.
5. Building the visible promotion portfolio (ADRs, fitness functions, governance, "paved roads").
